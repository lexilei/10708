\section*{Big Picture: Post-Midterm Roadmap}
% ============================================================

The central problem of PGMs: distributions are \textbf{intractable}---sampling, computing marginals, and evaluating the partition function $Z$ are all \#P-hard in general (and interreducible).

The second half of the course addresses this with two questions:

\subsection*{Question 1: How to do inference on an intractable distribution?}

Two families of approximate inference:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{MCMC} (L9, L11): Design a Markov chain whose stationary distribution is the target $\pi$. We never need to compute $Z$---only unnormalized density \emph{ratios} (e.g.\ the MH acceptance ratio $\frac{\tilde{p}(x')q(x|x')}{\tilde{p}(x)q(x'|x)}$ where $Z$ cancels).
  \item \textbf{Variational Inference} (L13): Turn inference into optimization. Find $q^* = \arg\min_{q \in \mathcal{Q}} \mathrm{KL}(q \| p)$, equivalently maximize the ELBO:
  \[
    \mathrm{ELBO}(q) = H(q) + \mathbb{E}_{q}[E(x)] = \log Z - \mathrm{KL}(q\|p) \leq \log Z
  \]
\end{itemize}

\subsection*{Question 2: How to \emph{learn} (train) a generative model?}

Different strategies, with increasing departure from classical MLE:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{EM} (L15): For latent-variable models. Uses VI as a subroutine---alternate between E-step (approximate posterior via ELBO) and M-step (update $\theta$). This is coordinate ascent on the ELBO.
  \item \textbf{VAE} (L16): Scale EM to neural nets. Amortized inference $q_\phi(z|x)$ + reparameterization trick to backprop through sampling.
  \item \textbf{GAN} (L17--18): Abandon likelihood entirely. VAE produces blurry samples because $\mathrm{KL}(p\|q)$ encourages mode-averaging. GANs instead use an adversarial game---a discriminator learns the ``distance'' between real and generated distributions.
  \item \textbf{Score Matching} (L19): Also abandon $Z$, but differently. Match the \emph{score function} $\nabla_x \log p_\theta(x)$ directly. Since $\nabla_x \log \frac{e^{-E_\theta(x)}}{Z} = -\nabla_x E_\theta(x)$, the partition function $Z$ vanishes under the gradient.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{remark}
  MCMC and VI are both \emph{inference} methods (given a model, answer queries about it). EM/VAE/GAN/Score Matching are \emph{learning} methods (train the model). EM and VAE use inference as a subroutine; GAN and Score Matching bypass inference entirely.
\end{remark}
